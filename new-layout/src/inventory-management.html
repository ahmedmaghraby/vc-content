Define: Inventory Management

What is inventory management?

Answer

A part of supply chain management, inventory management (or stock management) is the process of managing a business' inventory and stock. To further define inventory management, we can note that it includes aspects like the storage of inventory, overseeing orders for stock inventory, and controlling the number of items in stock.

How can you determine the right amount
of product to hold in inventory?

Getting the right amount of inventory when stocking is extremely important. Order too little and you're at risk of losing customers. Order too much and you might be stuck with extra stock that you'll be forced to sell at a big discount or have it expire; all while paying extra carrying costs.

One way to go about deciding on a reordering amount is to use information from previous months. In this case, you need to have the right inventory data at hand. A smart choice here would be to get a solution that will automatically track your inventory fluctuations. Even if you chose to go a slightly different route and use inventory forecasting software or even just Excel, you're still going to need inventory information from the previous months.

  • Fixed cost per year
  • Demand in units per year
  • Carrying cost per unit per year

Purchasing the right amount of inventory stock is only the beginning. It's always important to keep carrying costs in mind, which are attached to all products you buy. The larger the inventory you have, the more you'll be spending on storage, while increasing your risk of having products going out-of-date. Good news is there is an equation that helps you with this - the Economic Order Quantity (EOQ) formula.

Economic Order Quantity is a formula that helps calculate how many units your business should be adding to its inventory in order to reduce the total costs of inventory management â€“ including factors like order costs, holding costs, and shortage costs. There are several EOQ calculators you can find online to assist in your calculations.

How to set the reorder point for new inventory

The next important point you need to calculate is the right time to place your order for inventory restocking. EOQ answers the question of "What is an inventory level you want to maintain?", but it doesn't tell you how to actually restock. Ideally, you want your restock shipment to arrive when your previous batch is about to sell out. If it comes too early, you might face all kinds of storage and handling issues. And if it comes late, you're in danger of running out of stock. So, you want to make sure you always have enough products, which is where determining your reorder point gets important.
In order to do it right, you would need to know the time it takes for
  • Picking and handling of your items
  • Packing
  • Shipping (lead time)

Also, having safety stock is always a good idea!

What is the Best Tool for Inventory Management?

Many small businesses rely on excel spreadsheets for their inventory management, but once your business starts growing, they become extremely limiting. To save yourself the trouble of dealing with all sorts of stock-related issues, look for a cloud-based inventory management system that tracks inventory movement across all your sales channels in real time. It will significantly reduce your risk of overselling.
One of the main advantages of switching to inventory management software is automation of the whole process - you won't have to worry about overselling by accident or missing reorder points. It will also help reduce the likelihood of human error. Plus, it will save you a significant amount of time that you could allocate to growing your business.
